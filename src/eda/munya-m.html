7.1 Research Question:

How do early and mid-career pay levels vary across U.S. states, and what roles do STEM enrollment and alumni perception (making the world better) play in this variation?

7.2 Why this is important?

Career Planning for Students: students can make informed decisions about which schools or states offer better long-term financial prospects Especially relevant for students deciding between STEM and non-STEM paths.

Education Policy and Funding: It highlights states or institutions that produce high-earning graduates, which could inform public investment or scholarship programs.

STEM and Social Impact Balance: It examines whether higher STEM enrollment aligns with better salary outcomes — and whether that comes at the expense of perceived social impact; promoting discussion on balancing career success with societal contributions.

Economic Mobility and Equality: Understanding salary outcomes across states helps assess regional disparities in economic opportunity.It may inform state-level efforts to improve education outcomes and workforce development.
